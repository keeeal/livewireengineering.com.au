About me

From the age of ten I was captivated by prototype sports cars, especially Le Mans. I had mechanic for a father and quickly gained skills in electronics. My youth was spent in sheds and on farms - making things, breaking things or finding out how things worked.

After completing year 12, I got a job restoring MGs with some very wise, old-school mechanics. They taught me as much about taking pride in my work and attention to detail as they taught me about the nuts and bolts. I developed skills in business management and team leadership.

An apprenticeship as a mechanic with the Motor Traders Association gave me a well-rounded experience of the automotive industry. I received professional training and was exposed to small service stations, dealerships, mobile mechanics and one-make specialists.

During my time as an apprentice I took an unpaid after-hours job with Ford Motorsport. Guided by two mechanics, I managed the construction and competition of a car that won its class in the Nation Rally Championships. Jump forward 10 years and I was living in the United Kingdom working across Europe as a race mechanic - on the Le Mans cars I saw as a child!

What this means for you

Opportunities have led me to work on a huge variety of both electrical and mechanical equipment including freight trains, boats, farm machinery, industrial computers and mining equipment to name a few. In more recent years I have been employed to oversee and coordinate large projects, like the installation of electronic communication equipment, mobile data, tracking, satellite phones and radios in ambulances.

Many years in many industries, being attentive and a genuine fascination in the technical world has given me confidence, creativity, resourcefulness and the ability to make decisions under pressure. I take 25 years knowledge and experience to work each day, along with the ability to understand customers needs and help them reach their requirements in the most efficient manner.
